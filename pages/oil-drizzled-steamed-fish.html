Oil Drizzled Steamed Fish

Total cooking time: 25 mins | Serves 2
Any large fish will do for this easy and delicious dish. If you catch your own, tell your friends to cut out the backseat fishing.
  • 10.5 oz fillet of fresh white saltwater fish, skin on (300g)
  • 1/4 thumb of ginger, sliced
  • 1 thumb of ginger, cut into needles
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (15mL)
  • 1/2 tsp shaoxing wine (2.5mL)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (5mL), or to taste.
  • parsley, for garnish
  • scallions, shredded, for garnish
  1. Pat fillet dry and make some incisions at 45 degrees into the fish and stick the sliced ginger in. Marinate for about 15 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare your steamer.
  2. Put the fish on a dish, skin up. Top with ginger needles. Steam on high heat for about 8-10 mins.
  3. Mix the soy sauce and shaoxing wine together and pour over the fish.
  4. Put the fish back into the steamer and steam for about another 5 minutes, or until the fish is done. You might want to put a toothpick through the thickest part of the fish - if there is equal resistance all throughout the fillet and it comes out cleanly, itâ€™s done!
  5. Drizzle the sesame oil over evenly. Remove the ginger slices (for aesthetic purposes) and top with the parsley and scallions (if any). Cover the steamer again for another 10 seconds to cook the greens slightly to remove some of the bitterness.
  6. Eat with rice!
